\begin{center}
   {\bf\Large Voronoi-Based Surface Generation from Image Masks}
\end{center}

\vspace{1.5cm}
\begin{center} 
{\bf\large Omar Hafez, Mark Rashid}\\

%\vspace{5mm}
{Department of Civil \& Environmental Engineering}\\
{University of California, Davis}\end{center}

\vspace{5mm}

\begin{center}
{\bf Provisional Journal: Graphical Models (Elsevier)}
\end{center}

\vspace{10mm}
\abstract{A novel approach is presented that generates surface meshes (or b-reps) from image masks of MRI or CT scans. Provided that an image is segmented into a binary image mask, the method locally approximates the material interface by minimizing the relative error in moments of volume between the voxelated image mask and the volume enclosed by an approximating template surface. Voronoi partitioning is employed to perform surface reconstruction on the resulting oriented point cloud. Namely, Voronoi sites are placed on either side of each point along its orientation, Voronoi partitioning is performed, and facets who share Voronoi sites of differing material ID are extracted. The Voronoi approach guarantees watertightness of the resulting b-rep. The method is shown to perform competitively in honoring shape and volume metrics of an underlying canonical image mask when compared to commercial software, as well as qualitatively for a variety of real examples. The approach has proven to provide robust, high-quality data that may be inserted into an image-based meshing pipeline for the purposes of physics-based simulation, 3D printing, or visualization applications.The framework also allows for future introduction of additional template interfaces, which open the door for honoring sharp edges and corners, high-curvature regions, and multi-material interfaces.}
